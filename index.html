DASHBOARD

Welcome to your plant dashboard!

This dashboard provides an overview of the temperature, humidity, light value, and soil moisture conditions for your plant box over the past week. You can use this information to make sure your plant is getting the ideal environment it needs to thrive. To learn more about your plant's ideal growing conditions, please consult the plant care guide for your specific species. We hope you enjoy using your plant dashboard! If you have any questions or feedback, please don't hesitate to contact us.

Dashboard Page

This dashboard displays temperature, humidity, light value, soil moisture, project status, and the number of temperature and humidity interruptions. The first graph displays the daily averages for temperature, humidity, light value, and soil moisture. The second graph depicts the daily average number of temperature and humidity interruptions. In other words, the dashboard provides an overview of your plant box's environmental conditions and project status, as well as any potential problems that may arise.

SOIL SETTING

Soil Set-up

The Soil Setup Form is a form that allows you to enter information about the soil you are using. This information can be used to help you determine the best way to fertilize your plants and improve the quality of your soil.












Soil setup List

After clicking "Add Soil Details," your input in the Soil Setup Form gets submitted to the system, where it's stored in a database as a new soil entry. This entry appears in the Soil Setup List, allowing you to view, edit, or delete it using the Actions column. This data helps in optimizing plant fertilization and soil quality improvement.

Soil setup Form

The form includes the following fields:
• Soil Name: The name of the soil type.
• N(Nitrogen) Details: Information about the nitrogen content of the soil. Nitrogen is essential for plant growth and development. It also improves the quality of fruits and vegetables
• P(Phosphorus) Details: Information about the phosphorus content of the soil. Phosphorus is essential for root development, flowering, and fruiting.
• K(Potassium) Details: Information about the potassium content of the soil. Potassium is essential for water regulation, nutrient transport, and disease resistance. It also helps to strengthen cell walls and make plants more tolerant of stress.
• Soil Description: A general description of the soil, including its texture, structure, and drainage characteristics.

CROP SETTING

Crop Setup Form

IDEAL ENVIRONMENTAL SETTING

Ideal Environment Setup

The "Ideal Environment Setup" page enables you to configure the ideal environment for your crops to thrive in. For your crops to develop, remain healthy, and yield a lot, the ideal environment is essential.

Ideal Environment Setup List

A list of the ideal plant environments that users have configured is displayed by the Ideal Environment Setup List feature. It acts as a summary and quick reference for the different setups designed for various plants or crops.

Ideal Environment Setup Form

Here, you can adjust a number of parameters to create the ideal environment for your unique plants.
Ideal Environment Name - Give the ideal environment setup a name or identifier. This name aids users in distinguishing between different configurations.
Select Soil - Using a dynamic combo box, you can choose the correct soil type. The combo box displays a list of available options, which can change depending on the Soil Setup selected.
Select a Crop – From a dynamic combo box, you can choose the right crop. Depending on the Crop Setup provided, the combo box displays a list of available options.
Enter Ideal Temperature – For the plant box to have the best conditions for growth, set the ideal temperature.
Enter Ideal Humidity - Set the desired level of humidity, which is essential to plant health.
Enter Ideal Soil Moisture - Determine the ideal moisture content of the soil for the plants.
Enter Ideal Tank Water Level - Set the tank's ideal water level because water is necessary for plant growth.

CHARTS

Charts

The charts show the hourly progress of a project, and can be filtered by project and interval type. To use the charts, first select a project from the drop-down menu. Then, select the interval type you want to view. The default interval is "Hourly", but you can also view the progress by day, week, or month. Overall, the project charts are a valuable tool for evaluators. They can be used to track the progress of projects, identify potential problems, and take steps to get projects back on track.
